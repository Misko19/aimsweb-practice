# Product and UX principles

## Two clear paths

Guest practice is useful without an account and stores history only in the browser. Parent tracking is adult-owned; children use a nickname, grade, and built-in avatar, never an email, legal name, exact birthdate, school, photo, location, or voice recording.

## Developmental design

- Pre-K–2: one decision at a time, short language, large targets, optional spoken prompts, adult-assisted oral work.
- Grades 3–5: concise explanations, visible progress, and limited cosmetic choice.
- Grades 6–12: calmer language and styling, denser information, full keyboard support, and no babyish rewards.
- Accessibility settings are never locked to age. Reduced motion, audio, large targets, and clear focus remain available to everyone.

## Healthy motivation

Celebrate finite sessions, persistence, and personal improvement. Do not use broken streaks, leaderboards, random rewards, currencies, infinite play, autoplay, nagging notifications, or loss-framed prompts. Practice is untimed by default except when timing is the actual skill being practiced.

## Accessibility baseline

Target WCAG 2.2 AA: semantic structure, keyboard operation, visible focus, non-color status cues, at least 44px product touch targets, responsive reflow, 200% zoom, reduced-motion support, stable timers, and written alternatives for audio.

## Privacy baseline

This is intentionally child-directed, so product decisions assume COPPA applies. Guest mode sends no progress data to a server. Child-facing pages contain no ads, behavioral analytics, session replay, social widgets, or third-party fonts. Parent controls must include review, correction, export, per-child deletion, and account deletion. Public launch requires qualified privacy/legal review and a verified parental-consent approach.
